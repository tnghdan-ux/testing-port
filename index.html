About Me

Designing a 0-to-1 Talent Funnel for Internship Program 2024

TymeX had not built a strong young talent pipeline for over two years. I helped design and execute the end-to-end internship program — from research to onboarding — to attract, assess, and convert quality candidates at scale.

User Insight Funnel Design Data Storytelling Stakeholder Management
Context

TymeX had not run a structured internship in over two years. Previous efforts suffered from low awareness, no assessment framework, and fragmented coordination. There was a clear need to design a scalable, candidate-friendly pipeline from scratch.

Problem
  • Under 50 applications in previous programs
  • Low brand awareness among target tech students
  • No structured assessment or evaluation criteria
  • Fragmented communication journey across stages
  • Inefficient coordination between hiring teams
My Role

End-to-end program owner — primary & secondary research → candidate persona → core competency framework → communication plan → hiring process & candidate journey → stakeholder management → ATS data cleaning, analysis, and retrospective reporting

The Whole Process That I Managed

I started by conducting primary research through candidate surveys and reviewing secondary internal reports to understand the internship market, the target candidate persona, and the key barriers that affected application intent.

From these insights, I partnered with the hiring team to define the core competency framework for assessment and shaped a communication plan to reach candidates with the right message, channel, and timing.

How Branding & Social Media Can Drive Hiring

Stakeholders across HR, Talent Acquisition, and Leadership needed to understand whether social media truly impacted hiring outcomes and how recruitment marketing influenced the candidate funnel. Raw data was not enough — it needed interpretation.

Data Analysis & Storytelling User Insight Funnel Design Stakeholder Management
Context

Stakeholders across HR, Talent Acquisition, and Leadership wanted to understand whether social media truly impacted hiring outcomes and how recruitment marketing influenced the candidate funnel. Raw platform data alone was not enough — it needed to be interpreted into business-friendly insights.

Problem
  • Does social media actually support hiring outcomes?
  • How does social media relate to careersite traffic?
  • Which content types create useful awareness?
  • What signals can predict future CV volume?
  • How do branding initiatives influence candidate conversion?

Insights Discovered
  • Careersite traffic can act as an early signal of future CV volume — traffic trends often preceded application spikes by 2–4 weeks.
  • Social media introduces new audiences before they search directly — brand exposure precedes organic Google search intent.
  • Candidate behavior includes a delay between awareness and application — multi-touch attribution matters more than last-click.
  • Recruitment marketing contributes to the funnel before the final conversion stage — its value is in pipeline priming, not direct conversion.

Impact & What I Learned

  • Improved stakeholder visibility into how social media connects to hiring
  • Clarified the role of recruitment marketing as a funnel contributor, not just a brand activity
  • Supported more informed channel and content planning decisions
  • Data storytelling means connecting scattered metrics into one coherent narrative — not just charting numbers

Workflow automation · Tracking system · Data visibility

Building an Internal Redirect & Tracking Tool

Our link tracking operations relied on Bitly, which created critical limitations around audience segmentation, redirect speed, and link reliability. I scoped and built an internal replacement using n8n.

Workflow Automation Data Analysis Funnel Design Product Communication
Problem

Our link tracking relied on Bitly — which was causing real operational pain. Redirects took 5–7 seconds, links expired after 4–5 months, and most critically, it couldn't distinguish between audience groups sharing the same destination link.

Pain Points
  • Single-destination: can't split audience tracking on shared links
  • 5–7 second redirect delay hurts user experience
  • Links expire after 4–5 months — reliability risk
  • No real-time click data or audience segmentation
  • Third-party dependency — no ownership or control
My Role

Identified the problem → Designed the redirect logic and link structure → Built the entire system in n8n → Automated link creation, tracking, and dashboard generation → Defined the value proposition and future roadmap

  • Problem scoping from user pain points to technical requirements
  • End-to-end ownership: identified problem → designed system → shipped tool
  • Scalability thinking: built for reuse, not just one campaign
  • Data visibility as a product feature — not just a reporting add-on

Through Communication

Tyme operates across multiple markets and teams. Product updates and feature releases were not always easy for non-product teams to understand. I built recurring communication formats that translated product into clear, meaningful narratives.

Product Communication Stakeholder Execution User Insight Product Value Translation
Context

Tyme operates across multiple markets and teams. Product updates, feature releases, and customer value were not always easy for non-product or non-technical teams to understand, creating gaps in alignment and internal visibility.

The Gap
